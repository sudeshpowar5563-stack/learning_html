GaneshUtsav's history spans from ancient worship to its modern form as a public festival popularized by freedom fighter Bal Gangadhar Tilak. While the worship of Ganesha dates back to ancient texts and the festival was celebrated privately in households for centuries, Tilak transformed it into a large-scale public event in 1893 to foster unity against British rule. This move allowed people to gather for religious and cultural purposes, making the festival a symbol of nationalist sentiment.
